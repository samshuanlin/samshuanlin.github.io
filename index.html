Trapped!…?

Wildlife Camera Trap Research

Research Overview

My research focuses on developing and deploying wildlife camera traps to study animal behavior and population dynamics in their natural habitats. This work combines computer vision, machine learning, and conservation biology to create non-invasive monitoring systems.

Technical Implementation

The camera trap system uses motion sensors to trigger high-resolution photography, capturing detailed images of wildlife without human interference. Advanced image processing algorithms help identify species and track individual animals over time.

Data collection and analysis provide insights into animal migration patterns, population densities, and behavioral changes in response to environmental factors.

Conservation Impact

This research contributes to conservation efforts by providing accurate, long-term data on wildlife populations. The non-invasive nature of camera traps ensures minimal disturbance to natural habitats while gathering crucial information for environmental protection initiatives.

CAN YOU ESCAPE?

Interactive Escape Room Project

Project Overview

"CAN YOU ESCAPE?" is an innovative escape room experience that combines physical puzzles with digital technology to create an immersive, interactive adventure. Players must work together to solve complex challenges and escape within a time limit.

Technical Features

The escape room incorporates motion sensors, RFID technology, LED lighting systems, and custom software to create dynamic puzzle experiences. Each puzzle is designed to test different skills: logic, teamwork, creativity, and technical knowledge.

Design Philosophy

The project emphasizes accessibility and inclusivity, ensuring that puzzles can be solved by people with diverse abilities and backgrounds. The experience promotes critical thinking, collaboration, and problem-solving skills in an engaging format.

Robotics Funsies

Stanford Funbotics Contributions

About Stanford Funbotics

Stanford Funbotics is a student-run organization dedicated to bringing STEM education to underresourced communities. Through hands-on robotics workshops and mentorship programs, we inspire the next generation of engineers and innovators.

My Role

As a volunteer with Stanford Funbotics, I design and lead robotics workshops for students in underresourced schools. My responsibilities include curriculum development, hands-on instruction, and mentoring students as they build and program their own robots.

Impact

Through these workshops, students gain practical experience with robotics, programming, and engineering concepts. Many participants discover a passion for STEM fields and develop confidence in their technical abilities. The program has reached hundreds of students across multiple schools in the Bay Area.

Workshop Topics

• Basic robotics concepts and components
• Programming fundamentals using block-based coding
• Sensor integration and autonomous navigation
• Problem-solving through iterative design
• Team collaboration and project management
